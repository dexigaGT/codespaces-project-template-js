RC Cyber Truck Project

Introduction

This project involved designing and building a remote-controlled cyber truck that could withstand high-impact crashes. The goal was to create a durable RC vehicle using advanced machining techniques and materials.

Background

RC cars are typically made from plastic or lightweight metals, but for this project, I wanted to create something much more robust. The cyber truck design was chosen for its angular, futuristic aesthetic and the challenge of machining complex geometries.

Design Process

Materials Used

  • Aluminum alloy for the chassis
  • Steel reinforcements
  • High-strength polymers for body panels
  • Brushless motor system

Machining Techniques

  • CNC milling for precision parts
  • Laser cutting for body panels
  • 3D printing for prototyping

Construction

The build process took several months and involved:

  1. CAD modeling of all components
  2. Machining metal parts to tight tolerances
  3. Assembly and testing
  4. Crash testing and iterations

Results

The final vehicle successfully withstood multiple high-speed crashes without significant damage. The project demonstrated the importance of material selection and engineering principles in RC vehicle design.

Conclusion

This project taught me valuable lessons in mechanical engineering, from design to manufacturing. The cyber truck serves as a testament to the power of combining traditional machining with modern design tools.
